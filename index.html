A Tribute to Edgar Allan Poe

Edgar Allan Poe was one of the most important fiction writers of America. As a representative of the Romantic School of literature, he started a sub-style of their own, when focused his writings on the fantastic and the macabre. He was born in Boston, United States. And lived from 1809 to 1849.

He is one of the fathers of the Gothic Fiction, and is also regarded as the creator of the detective tale. As such he influenced generations of writters from horror to science fiction. People like Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, Mary Shelley, H. P. Lovecraft, Sidney Sheldon, Agatha Cristie and so many others.

Altought a fiction writer, he was rigorous in his technique, be in short tales or poems. These were their main endeavours, as he wrote very few long stories. His tales of suspense and mistery keep readers thrilled all the way to the conclusion

Another particularity of his work were their characters. While most horror authors put normal characters in a strange world full of dangers, Poe created paranoid and strange characters in a normal world. Much of the horror is psychological, and the only true danger to the characters is the slow descent into madness their strange passion and mania drive them to. Maybe a reflex of their own turbulent life, marked by depression and various tragic events. He died young, and his death is surrounded with mistery.

A Timeline of life, works and unfortunate events

19 January 1809
He was born in Boston, the son of two actors, Elizabeth Arnold Hopkins Poe and David Poe Jr.
1810
His father abandoned the family. His mother would die a year later. He was adopted by a rich merchant, Jonh Allan, and his wife.
1826
Poe entered the Virginia University and studied Languages, but dropped a year later, after losing a lot of money in gambling debts. He gone to Boston, sustaining himself with odd jobs, and writing for newspapers
1827 - 1831 Military Carreer
Unable to support himself, he enlisted in the United States Army. During this period his foster mother died from a illness, and he wrote three books of poems. He distanced even more from his step father, and dropped out from the Army. His fellow cadets helped financing his book Poems, that included the poems The City In The Sea, Israfel and To Helen.
1931 - 1946 Publishing Career
Poe strived to make a live from writing only, he turned his attention to prose. He wrote his only drama The Politian, and tales for magazines and other periodicals. In 1935 he married his Cousin Virginia Clemm, three years later he published the collection Tales of Grotesque And Arabesque. He wrote the majority of his best known works in this period, like The Raven, and also got a good reputation as a literary critique.
1947 - 1949 From Virginia Clemm's Death to it's own
Poe's wife Virginia started showing symptoms of tuberculosis, the same illness that killed his mother, in 1942. She died in 1947. Poe became increasingly unstable after her death, and his drinking habits got worse. In 1949 Poe was found delirious, in the streets of Baltimore, in urgent need of medical help. His Death is surrounded by mystery, all medical records had been lost, including his death certificate.
